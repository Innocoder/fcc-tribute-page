Black Coffee

(DJ)

South Africa's Number One DJ

Short summary and Early career

Real name Nkosinathi Inno Maphumulo was born on 11 March 1976 in Umlazi, South Africa.
He moved to Umtata, the home town of Nelson Mandela, and grew up in Durban.
Maphumulo majored in Jazz Studies at Durban University of Technology before working as a backup singer.
He was chosen as one of two South African participants in the Red Bull Music Academy in 2003, jump-starting him into the South African dance music scene.

Music career and timeline:

  • 2005–2006: Black CoffeeLaunched his career with a remix of Hugh Masekela's 1972 hit Stimela. Later that year he released his self-titled debut album, and created his company.

  • 2007–2008: Have Another OneBy 2007, Black Coffee had become recognized in the South African DJ scene with tribal, vocal-laced beats. In the same year he released his second studio album titled Have Another One

  • 2009–2011: Home Brewed Black Coffee released his third album, Home Brewed, Between 2009 and 2010, new releases from Culoe de Song, Tumelo and the great Zakes Bantwini achieved gold and platinum sales.

  • 2012–2013: Africa Rising In 2012, Africa Rising DVD and triple-CD sold double platinum in a space of a month.

  • 2015–present: Pieces of Me After his world tour started in January 2015 at Circoloco in Mexico and ended in August 2015 at Circoloco at DC-10, he released his 5th album, Pieces Of Me.
